Learn how to create your first Tyform from scratch.

## Starting a New Form

1. Log in to your Tyform dashboard
2. Click the **"Create Form"** button
3. Choose how to start:
   - **Blank Form** — Start from scratch
   - **Template** — Use a pre-built template
   - **AI Generated** — Describe what you need

## The Form Builder Interface

<Callout type="info">
  The form builder shows a live preview of your form as you build it.
</Callout>

### Main Sections

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Question Panel** | Add and configure questions |
| **Preview** | See how your form looks |
| **Settings** | Configure form behavior |
| **Theme** | Customize appearance |

## Adding Your First Question

### Click "Add Question"

You'll see a list of question types. For your first form, try:

1. **Welcome Screen** — Greet your respondents
2. **Short Text** — Ask for their name
3. **Email** — Collect their email
4. **Multiple Choice** — Ask a qualifying question
5. **Thank You Screen** — Confirm submission

### Configuring Questions

Each question has these options:

- **Question Text** — The main question
- **Description** — Optional helper text
- **Required** — Make the question mandatory
- **Placeholder** — Example input text

## Writing Great Questions

<Callout type="tip">
  Keep questions short and conversational. One question at a time keeps respondents focused.
</Callout>

### Do's

- Use friendly, conversational language
- Be specific about what you're asking
- Add descriptions for clarity
- Use placeholders as examples

### Don'ts

- Ask multiple things in one question
- Use jargon or technical terms
- Make every question required
- Write long, confusing questions

## Form Settings

Navigate to the **Settings** tab:

### General Settings

- **Form Title** — Internal name for your form
- **Public Title** — Shown to respondents
- **Close Date** — Auto-close the form on a date
- **Response Limit** — Maximum responses allowed

### Submission Settings

- **Confirmation Message** — What users see after submitting
- **Redirect URL** — Send users to a custom page
- **Email Notifications** — Get notified of responses

## Saving and Publishing

### Auto-Save

Tyform automatically saves your changes as you work. Look for the "Saved" indicator.

### Publishing

When ready to share:

1. Click **"Publish"** in the top right
2. Review your form settings
3. Click **"Publish Form"**

Your form is now live and ready to share!

## Preview Before Publishing

Always preview your form:

1. Click the **Preview** button
2. Go through your form as a respondent
3. Check for typos and flow issues
4. Test on mobile view
